# Visa Page Redesign — Brainstorm Options

Date: 2026-03-17

## Context
The current visa page is too generic — too much whitespace, not personal enough, feels like a government form rather than a premium collectible. The page should have two layers:
1. **Visa card** — compact artifact, the showstopper visual
2. **Below the card** — scrollable magazine-style story weaving photos, lore, and debrief together

Participants should be **featured** — avatar row with aliases, like a "bearer" section.

## Approach A: "Stamped Passport Page" (SELECTED)
- Visa card becomes a dense, layered artifact — like a real passport page that's been through customs
- **Cover photo** fills more space as a bleed-to-edge cinematic band across the top, with VIZA title overlaid
- **Stamp** is large, overlaid at an angle (like physically stamped)
- **Participants** in a "BEARER" / "DELEGATION" row with avatars + aliases
- **Data fields** are tighter — no labels, just typographic hierarchy (city big, date smaller, duration as "5 DAYS")
- **One-liner** engraved at the bottom as a quote
- **Below card**: magazine layout — hero photo → lore with interspersed photos → classified debrief section
- Trade-off: Most ambitious. Requires careful layout to avoid clutter.

## Approach B: "Diplomatic Dossier"
- Cream card reorganized into two columns
- Left: structured data (city, flag, dates, participants)
- Right: cover photo + stamp overlapping
- Participants get a "DELEGATION" header with avatar + alias
- Below: same magazine flow with document-stamp-styled section dividers
- Trade-off: Safer but might still feel form-like.

## Approach C: "Travel Document Collage"
- Cover photo dominant (60% of card), data/stamp/participants layered over it
- Semi-transparent overlays, tape/sticker metaphors
- Very Instagram-friendly visual but more "scrapbook" than "passport"
- Trade-off: Visually striking but departs from passport metaphor.

## Key Decisions
- Primary context: Both artifact + story (layered — card is compact, scroll reveals full mission)
- Pain points: Too much whitespace (A), not personal enough (E)
- Below-card main event: All three woven together — photos, lore, debrief as magazine feature (D)
- Participant prominence: Featured — clear row with avatars and aliases (B)
